Protecting Our Planet

SDG 14 and SDG 15 are focused on protecting marine and terrestrial ecosystems, which are critical for sustaining life on Earth. Oceans and forests are vital for regulating the Earth's climate, providing food, and supporting biodiversity. However, overfishing, pollution, deforestation, and habitat destruction pose significant threats.

SDG 14 - Life Below Water:

SDG 14 aims to conserve and sustainably use the oceans, seas, and marine resources. Healthy oceans support livelihoods, biodiversity, and regulate the climate.

  • Reduce marine pollution caused by plastics and other waste.
  • Protect and restore marine ecosystems to promote biodiversity.
  • End overfishing and ensure sustainable fisheries management.
  • Minimize ocean acidification and its harmful impacts on marine life.
  • Support sustainable development of coastal and marine resources.

SDG 15 - Life on Land:

SDG 15 aims to halt deforestation, desertification, and loss of biodiversity to protect terrestrial ecosystems and promote sustainable land management.

  • Restore degraded forests and ecosystems to combat desertification.
  • Protect endangered species and conserve biodiversity hotspots.
  • Prevent illegal poaching and wildlife trafficking.
  • Promote sustainable agriculture to protect soil health and forests.
  • Integrate sustainable forest management into national policies.
