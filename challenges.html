Challenges Facing Our Planet

SDG 13: Climate Action

Climate change is escalating. Average global temperatures have increased by over 1Â°C since pre-industrial times. This has led to more frequent and intense hurricanes, floods, droughts, and wildfires. Many nations lack the funding or infrastructure to adapt to these rapid changes. Political inaction and economic barriers also slow down global climate efforts.

SDG 14: Life Below Water

Marine ecosystems are under threat. Over 8 million tons of plastic enter the oceans every year. Coral reefs are dying due to ocean acidification and warming waters. Overfishing has depleted nearly 90% of the world's fish stocks. Lack of regulation and illegal fishing make it harder to protect marine life and ensure sustainable harvests.

SDG 15: Life on Land

Our forests, animals, and biodiversity are vanishing. Over 10 million hectares of forests are lost each year, mainly due to agriculture and development. Wildlife populations have dropped by over 60% in the past 50 years. Invasive species, habitat loss, and illegal poaching all contribute to the degradation of our land-based ecosystems.
